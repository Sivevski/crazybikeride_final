Tour:Vienna-Split

Day1: Vienna-Bad Vöslau

In the above picture is  the situation in which my bike was when i first arrived in hotel Meininger  on  18.05.2018.
My plan was to assemble the bike and get it ready for an early morning departure next day. But it was not so simple. Besides  messing up the brakes, i realized that my newly bought  Vodafone  sim card is not functioning and i don’t have Internet. It was essential that i have internet since i didn’t bring any paper maps and without it, i  could simply not find my way. I spent almost  the whole morning trying to connect the sim card but it was useless and i went to the shop and bought a new one.
Vienna has a territory of around 400  square kilometers and it took me more than 1 hour just to get out of the city. It was clear  that i can not reach far that day.

My major concern during the whole trip in Austria was to follow the Eurovelo 9 route which is an exclusive road for bicycles that starts from Scandinavia and ends in Greece. It turned out that in many places it was a gravel road and not  asphalt like i expected. My bike was not fully fit for this kind of surface and as a result a had a few tire punctures during the trip.
In Germany and Austria you can see many so called bike gardens where local people come with their bikes and drink beer.
I gave my every effort to reach Wiener Neustadt that day but i was short for around 20-30 km and decided that it was time to get some food and find a place to sleep.
I was very lucky to find the only camp in that area. This was the first time that i used my tent and i had difficulties setting it up. The camp owner  helped me somehow and i was ready for sleep. I am wandering what could have been like, if i decided instead of camp , to do a wild camping that night? Since i could not  set the tent alone ,there was a big chance that i would freeze during the night because  it turned out that although it was  the month of May, the temperatures were close to zero degrees  and even though  i put on me everything i had as clothes ,i still  was not warm in the tent. Luckily i had no health problems the next morning. The day was sunny and  i felt fresh and ready to go on.
